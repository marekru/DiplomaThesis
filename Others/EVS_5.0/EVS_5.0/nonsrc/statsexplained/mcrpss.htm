THE MEAN CONTINUOUS RANKED PROBABILITY SKILL SCORE

The Mean Continuous Ranked Probability Skill Score () measures the performance of one forecasting system relative to another in terms of the Continuous Ranked Probability Score (). The measures the average square error of a probability forecast across all possible event thresholds. The comprises a ratio of the for the forecasting system to be evaluated (the "main forecasting system"), , over the for the reference forecasting system,

As a measure of average square error in probability, values for the approaching zero are preferred. It follows that a closer to 1 is preferred, as this indicates a low of the main forecasting system relative to the of the reference forecasting system. Unlike the , the is not "strictly proper" (i.e. it can be hedged). Also, the may behave erratically for forecasts of rare events because their errors of probability are necessarily small and their sampling uncertainties are likely high.
